# FURQAN Academy — Design Context

## Users

**Primary audience:** Universal — *all ages, all people*. This is the Quran; the platform doesn't segment by demographic. Children sit alongside elders; new reverts alongside lifelong students of tajweed. The interface must work for a 7-year-old's first juz' as gracefully as for a hāfiz preparing for ijāzah.

**Bilingual reality:** Arabic-first, English-secondary. Most students value Arabic display even if they're more comfortable reading English. RTL is the default direction; LTR is opt-in via the language toggle.

**Three secondary roles:** **Teachers** (admin-appointed, manage availability + sessions + grading), **Admins** (platform operations), **Moderators** (CV review, audit, intervention). Each role has its own dashboard but shares the same shell + design language.

**Job to be done:** "I want to find a trustworthy Quran teacher, book a session that fits my schedule, complete my homework, and see my progress over time — without ever feeling rushed, sold to, or watered down."

## Brand Personality

**Three words: Premium · Refined · Authentic**

- **Premium** — Apple-grade finish applied to a religious context. Materials feel intentional (glass, gold, cream); type rendering is crisp; spacing is generous. The platform earns trust through evident craft.
- **Refined** — Restraint over flash. No unnecessary ornament, no aggressive gradients, no SaaS-conversion patterns. Every visual choice answers "why is this here?"
- **Authentic** — Genuine connection to Quranic tradition without costume. Arabic typography is primary, not decorative. References to halaqa, ijāzah, talaqqi appear in their natural language. The platform does not perform piety; it serves it.

**Voice:** Warm but grave. Encouraging without cheerleading. Speaks of the Quran with the dignity it deserves — never breezy, never preachy. English copy mirrors the tone of classical translation footnotes: precise, calm, unembellished.

**Emotional goal:** **Confidence and reverence** — when a student opens the app, they should feel "this is a serious, beautifully made tool worthy of the work I'm doing." Progress is celebrated quietly, not gamified.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Premium dark theme with Islamic warmth. Deep blacks (`#0F0F0F`) with refined gold (`#C8A652`) accents — feels like a beautifully bound Quran with gold-foil lettering. Cards float on dark surfaces with subtle elevation.

**Islamic identity:** SVG geometric patterns (`islamic-pattern` class), ornamental gold dividers (`ornament-divider`, `gold-line`), and section accent gradients that evoke the warmth of a mosque interior. These are integral to the brand — subtle, never cluttered.

**Motion & animation:** Embrace purposeful motion. Shimmer effects on stats (`stat-shimmer`), slow pulse on CTAs (`animate-pulse-slow`), toast slide-ins, button press feedback (`neu-btn` scale). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — all motion disables gracefully.

**Theme:** Three modes — **dark** (current default), **light** (warm cream), and **auto** (follows system preference).

### Dark mode (current)
- Background `#0F0F0F`, Surface `#1A1A1A`, Cards `#1A1A1A` with `#2A2A2A` borders
- Foreground `#F5F0E8` (warm off-white), Muted `#9C9488`
- Gold `#C8A652` — rich and luminous on dark backgrounds

### Light mode (planned)
- Background `#FAF7F2` (warm cream), Cards `#FFFFFF` with `#E8E2D9` borders
- Foreground `#1a1510` (warm near-black), Muted `#8a7e6d`
- Gold `#B8922D` — darker and richer for proper contrast on light backgrounds

### Auto mode
- Follows `prefers-color-scheme` media query, toggling between dark and light tokens

**References (blend of four families):**
- **Quran apps** — Tarteel, Muslim Pro, Ayat. Reverent green/gold palettes, Arabic-first typography, classical mushaf cues. *Take from these:* spiritual gravitas, Arabic typographic care, restraint with religious imagery.
- **Apple iOS native** — Reminders, Books, Notes. Soft glass surfaces, generous padding, subtle micro-interactions, system-quality typography. *Take from these:* premium materials, gentle motion, content-first hierarchy.
- **Linear / Notion** — cool minimal SaaS. Whitespace as a feature, monochrome with one accent, restrained iconography. *Take from these:* density restraint on dashboards, command-bar polish, "nothing extra" composition.
- **Khan Academy / Coursera** — educational warmth. Approachable colors, clear progress indicators, encouragement-oriented copy. *Take from these:* visible-progress patterns and copy warmth — without the cartoon mascots.

**Anti-references (explicit):**
- ❌ **Generic LMS / corporate-training look** — stock photos, clip-art badges, dashboards crammed with widgets. The recent /student/dashboard pivot to airy density (lavender body + white app card + 4-KPI grid) is the antidote.
- ❌ **Childish / cartoonish styling** — bright primaries, mascot characters, comic illustrations. Even though children use this, the brand stays dignified; kids rise to the design.
- ❌ **Aggressive marketing / SaaS conversion patterns** — pop-up upsells, FOMO timers, gradient-everything CTAs, "Limited time!" copy. The Quran context demands restraint; a single calm CTA is enough.
- ❌ **Imagery of human faces / portraits in religious contexts** — no stock photography of people praying, reciting, or in worship contexts. Use abstract, calligraphic, or geometric imagery (Islamic patterns, mushaf pages, gold ornament) instead. Teacher avatars in profiles are fine; staged "spiritual people" photography is not.

**Composition shorthand for new surfaces:**
- Dark mode → keep the gold-on-black "Liquid Glass" identity (this is furqan's signature; reads premium-traditional).
- Light mode → adopt the airy reference grammar where it fits (lavender body, white app-shell, generous whitespace, 48px bold KPI numerals, simple flat progress bars). The student dashboard skin (`.student-dashboard-skin`) is the canonical example.
- Both modes share the same component vocabulary (StatCard, WidgetCard, BreakdownBar, DataTable, AnalyticsChart) — only tokens differ.

**Typography:**
- **Display/headings:** Amiri (`--font-display`) — a beautiful Arabic serif with Islamic character
- **Body:** Cairo (`--font-body`) — clean, modern, excellent Arabic+Latin rendering
- Arabic text is primary and bold; English is secondary in `text-xs text-muted`

**Layout:** RTL-first (`dir="rtl"` on root). Card-based with generous spacing. Max-width containers (`max-w-4xl` / `max-w-5xl` / `max-w-7xl` for public). Responsive grid (1-col mobile → multi-col desktop).

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every element must be immediately understandable. If a user needs to think about what a button does, the design has failed. Arabic labels first, English hints second.

2. **Progress is visible — quietly** — Students should always see where they are in their Quran journey. Stats, session counts, and quality ratings should be prominent but never gamified. No streak fireworks, no badge animations, no "Level up!" toasts. The progress bar fills calmly; the number ticks up; the work continues.

3. **Gold means action** — Gold (`#C8A652` dark / `#B8922D` light) is reserved for interactive elements: buttons, links, active states, focus rings. It should never appear on static/decorative elements. When a user sees gold, they know they can tap it. *(Skin variants — e.g. student-dashboard-skin's blue brand recolor — follow the same rule with their substituted accent.)*

4. **Respect the content** — The Quran is sacred content. Surah names, ayah references, and recitation terminology must be presented with typographic care. Never truncate Quran references. Session types and recitation standards always show their Arabic names. Never illustrate worship with stock photography.

5. **Motion with purpose** — Animations guide attention and reward interaction: shimmer on stats, pulse on CTAs, slide-in toasts, press feedback on buttons. Every motion must have a reason. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — all animations disable gracefully.

6. **Accessible by default** — High contrast ratios, large touch targets (WCAG 2.5.5 AAA — `.btn-md` ≥ 44px), visible focus rings (`2px solid gold`), screen-reader-friendly labels, full RTL support, and graceful degradation for slow connections.

7. **Restraint as a feature** — When in doubt, remove. One CTA per surface. One brand color per surface (gold, or skin substitute). Whitespace is not empty space — it is silence around the work. The reference for "is this too much?" is a well-bound mushaf, not a SaaS landing page.

## Component Patterns

**Primary Button:** `rounded bg-gold px-4 py-2 text-sm font-medium text-white` — dark text on gold, `neu-btn` for press feedback
**Primary CTA:** `rounded-full bg-primary px-8 py-3 text-lg font-semibold text-white neu-btn` — hero-level, can add `animate-pulse-slow`
**Card:** `rounded-2xl border border-card-border bg-card p-6`
**Input:** `w-full rounded-xl border border-input-border bg-input neu-inset px-4 py-2.5` + focus: `border-input-focus ring-1 ring-input-focus`
**Gold Badge:** `rounded-full border border-gold/30 bg-gold/10 px-2 py-0.5 text-xs text-gold`
**Status Badge:** `rounded-full border px-2 py-0.5 text-xs` + semantic color variants
**Error Alert:** `rounded-xl border border-error/30 bg-error/10 px-4 py-3 text-sm text-error`
**Toast:** Slide-in from left, backdrop blur, auto-dismiss 4s, semantic color borders
**Elevation:** `elevation-1` through `elevation-4` (increasing shadow depth on dark backgrounds)
**Decorative:** `islamic-pattern` (bg), `gold-line` (divider), `ornament-divider` (section break), `section-accent` (gradient bg)

## Icons

Lucide React — consistent stroke style, `size={18-24}`, gold (`text-gold`) for decorative/heading icons, `text-muted` for secondary actions.
